Chef · Owner

Corey Lee

Born in Seoul and raised in the United States, Corey Lee spent nine years as Chef de Cuisine under Thomas Keller at The French Laundry and Per Se before opening Benu in 2010. His cooking interweaves Korean foodways — often overlooked at this level of dining — with Cantonese and Japanese technique, built on California's produce and seafood.

The result is a menu of small, exacting courses: familiar shapes carrying unexpected flavor, served with the calm, unhurried attention of a kitchen that has spent over a decade refining every gesture.

The Room

A converted newspaper house

Benu occupies the ground floor of a building raised in 1922 as headquarters for the San Francisco Newspaper Company. Guests enter through a small, ivy-lined courtyard lined with vessels of soy sauce, left to age in the open air — a quiet threshold before the dining room.

Inside, the space is deliberately spare: no art, no flowers, cloth-less tables. The restraint is intentional, leaving full attention on the food and the exacting, unhurried service that carries it to the table.
